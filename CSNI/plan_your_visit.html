Step 1: Information gathering

Explore the ins-and-outs of the emerging medical tourism industry. Visit our medical tourism links to learn more about medical tourism and how it works, as well as read about financial, tax, and travel health considerations. Visit our hospitals links to locate providers abroad that offer the procedure you are seeking and compare provider credentials, costs, and services. Use our news link to read, view, and hear media reports about medical tourism as it continues to grow and develop as an industry.

Step 2: Consider the feasible treatment options

List down the different kinds of providers that exist, ranging from major hospitals to smaller clinics and private practices, as you decide what is the best fit for your medical needs. An alternative to working directly with a provider is to consider going through a health center like Cosmetic Surgery Nagpur India that operates as distinguised medical treatment and services. Such service providers often have established working relationships with a set number of providers covering a range of medical procedures.

Back to Step 2

Step 3: What all do wish to accomplish with your trip?

Do you intend this to be a strictly practical trip for medical purposes, or do you want to add a vacation alongside your health services? If its the former, then you can move ahead with your plan of action for getting the best possible services at the best price. If you want to add a further vacation element to your trip, enjoying the scenic beauty of India, you need to make additional plans. One would need to keep in mind the following factors: timing (before or after your procedure) seasonal factors (high or low tourist season and weather variables) visas (if beyond a standard tourist visa) additional costs and logistics of travel and accommodation

Back to Step 3

Step 4: Research and choose your destination

Irrespective of whether you are going for a medical procedure, or are going to top it up with a vacation on the side, it is worth your time to learn before hand about the country you are visiting! Give a thought to all these questions:

  1. Climate and terrain (is it rainy or dry season? What elevation will you be at?).
  2. Language competencies, how will you manage your communication, etc. English happens to be one of the official languages in India. Its widely spoken and understood in the metro cities.
  3. Cost of living (What is the range of prices for local accommodation? What does a meal, beer, or a cup of coffee cost?).
  4. Cost and means of travel (Are taxis, buses, trolleys, or other forms of public transport available? How expensive are they? How costly is it to rent a car? ).
  5. Public health and sanitary considerations?
  6. Public safety(Will you be in a large city? If so, what precautions should you take?).
  7. Local custom and etiquette.
  8. Local historical and tourist sites of interest.
  9. At the stage when you've settled on a destination, contact us and its customer care executives to begin planning and making the most of your trip.

Back to Step 4

Step 5: Plan out expenses

If the eventuality where you are planning to cover costs on your own, you need to carefully consider, compare, and calculate the various expenses that will arise in your medical travel. In addition to the cost of your health care procedures, these costs may include:

  1. Cost of passport and/or travel visas.
  2. Travel: Flights, airport pick-up, travel to-and-from medical provider. Transport for local sight seeing, etc.
  3. Hotel stay/Accommodations.
  4. Food and beverages.
  5. Phone calls, internet and other correspondence with family at home.
  6. Medications or medical supplies (crutches, etc.) for use during post-procedure recovery time.

Back to Step 5

Step 6: Chalk out dates of travel

Taking into account an advance notice period, present one or more possible dates for travel to us. Let the hospital reservations be worked out. Keep several factors in mind as you plan and arrange your travel and health care services:

  1. Peak travel dates to-and-from the country you will be visiting. The price for airline tickets and accommodations can vary dramatically.
  2. Weather conditions in your destination and how they might play into your post-op recovery and comfort.
  3. treatment and recovery-time requirements.

Back to Step 6

Step 7: Apply for passports and valid visas

All parties traveling should have valid passports with sufficient validity. Depending on your destination country, you may or may not be able to secure a tourist visa upon arrival at the airport. Be sure to determine ahead of time whether that is the case, or whether you need to arrange for a visa ahead of time with a representative Embassy or consular office.

Back to Step 7

Step 8: Organize records, patient history and other paperwork

Firstly, collate medical history, reports, prescriptions and other relevant documents at your end. Plan for how you are going to transfer or carry your medical records to you overseas medical provider, as well as for bringing records of your procedure home with you. In particular, you will need to decide whether to carry paper versions of your records with you or have them digitized and transferred electronically.

Back to Step 8

Step 9: Plan for post-procedure recovery.

The optimistic approach would be to hope is that all will go according to plan and schedule. Yet, even while anticipating a smooth procedure, one must still be diligent about planning for the recovery period. Likewise, for the sake of prudence and caution, one should anticipate possible complications and make arrangements ahead-of-time.

Some points to consider:

  1. How much recovery time will your procedure entail?
  2. If you will be hospitalized, for how long?

Standards of legal protection for patients vary dramatically from country to country. Once you have chosen a provider, do your best to educate yourself about what legal protections, if any, are guaranteed to you as a visiting patient. Consult with a country embassy or consular office before leaving, and use the web to research patient protection laws.

Back to Step 9

Step 10: Take your trip

Get healthy, travel to various parts of the mystic land of India, save money, and come back ready to share your experiences with others.
